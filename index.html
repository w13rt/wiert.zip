Swiss Design Principles

What is Swiss Design?

Swiss Design, also known as the International Typographic Style, is a design approach that emerged in Switzerland during the 1950s. It emphasizes clarity, simplicity, and grid-based layouts, often incorporating sans-serif typography and the use of white space to create a visually appealing, minimalistic aesthetic.

Key Elements

The Swiss style is defined by several key principles, including clean layouts, the use of a grid system, and a focus on typographic hierarchy. Colors are often minimal, with bold contrasts and strong visual elements. It also prioritizes functionality over decorative embellishments.
